What is AGISystem2?

AGISystem2 is a research project exploring formal reasoning engines that complement probabilistic AI systems. It implements mechanisms inspired by deliberative reasoning (sometimes called "System 2 thinking" in cognitive science) using hyperdimensional computing as the representational substrate.

The goal is not to replace neural networks, but to provide a complementary layer for scenarios where traceability, verifiability, and explainability are requirements, not nice-to-haves.

Research Project: AGISystem2 is an open-source research project, not a finished product. It demonstrates that deterministic, mathematically grounded AI reasoning is practical for specific domains. We make no claims about artificial general intelligence or solving fundamental AI problems.

Positioning: What Problem Does This Solve?

For applications where verification is critical—compliance, auditing, legal decisions, safety-critical systems—probabilistic pattern matching alone may not suffice. AGISystem2 addresses this gap:

AGISystem2 is not an LLM and does not try to be. It is a complementary reasoning engine designed for scenarios where complete traceability and formal verification are requirements, not options.

Scope & Limitations

What AGISystem2 does well:

  • Taxonomic reasoning (IS_A hierarchies, transitive inference)
  • Rule-based inference with backward chaining
  • Explicit contradiction detection within formalized domains
  • Complete audit trails for every conclusion

What AGISystem2 does NOT do:

  • Natural language understanding (requires external NL‚ÜíDSL translation)
  • Creative generation or open-ended conversation
  • Learning from unstructured data
  • Handling domains that haven't been formally modeled

Fundamental limitation: The system reasons correctly within a theory, but cannot guarantee the theory itself is correct. See The Symbol Grounding Problem for an honest discussion of this epistemological limitation.

  • Automatic scaling to arbitrary domains

The Meta-Rational Approach

We adopt a pragmatic stance: theories are tools, not truths. AGISystem2 guarantees correct reasoning within a theory, not the correctness of the theory itself. This isn't a limitation to apologize for—it's how all formal systems work, from mathematics to legal codes.

The value proposition is practical: transform natural language specifications into formal theories that can be analyzed, debugged, and revised. This doesn't solve the grounding problem, but it makes specifications explicit and their consequences computable.
