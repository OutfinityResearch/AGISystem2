What is AGISystem2?

AGISystem2 is an AI research platform inspired by the System 2 concept from cognitive psychology—the slow, deliberate, logical mode of human reasoning (as opposed to System 1's fast, intuitive responses). Born from European research initiatives, the platform brings together formal verification, neural-symbolic hybrid reasoning, and hyperdimensional computing.

A key innovation is the introduction of a semantic programming language (DSL) that gives formal, machine-verifiable meaning to structured fragments of language. Instead of teaching humans to write in formal logic, we created a language that captures the semantic structure implicit in common reasoning patterns. The result is machine-checkable reasoning over formally modeled content (with proofs/traces and deterministic evaluation coverage).

The Vision: Universal Theory Engine (UTE)

AGISystem2 is driven by a research hypothesis: a plausible route toward ‚Äúreal‚Äù AGI goes through the ability to represent and manipulate theories as computational objects—compositional, queryable, checkable, and revisable.

We call this target Universal Theory Engine (UTE): a platform where scientific and operational knowledge can be encoded as executable theories, verified by proofs/traces, revised under new evidence, and used to plan experiments. This connects directly to the project name: ‚ÄúSystem 2‚Äù as deliberate, auditable reasoning over explicit models.

The practical goal is not a single monolithic super-agent. It is a family of domain-specialized agents that can review, argue, prove, and automate daily scientific work—with auditability. We are still far from that end state. Getting there likely requires a community effort: LLMs can propose, but formal models and evaluation must judge.

What You Can Do With It (Today)

Build checkable micro-theories

Encode structured facts and rules in a DSL, query/prove them, and get explainable traces. This is the ‚Äúunit of progress‚Äù toward UTE: small theories that are executable, inspectable, and testable.

Compare reasoning substrates

Explore multiple HDC/VSA strategies under one contract (dense/sparse/metric/EMA/EXACT), and study scaling behavior. This is essential for capacity, decoding, and fast retrieval research.

Run reproducible experiments

Benchmarks and evaluation suites make progress measurable (correctness + performance) across strategies and reasoning priorities. Capacity experiments (saturation) stress-test ‚Äútheory as vector‚Äù representations.

Use LLMs safely (as proposers)

LLMs can translate, propose patches, and propose theory fragments. AGISystem2‚Äôs job is to validate and harden those proposals with eval data, proofs/traces, and reproducible workflows.

Why This Matters

For applications where verification is critical—compliance, medical decisions, legal reasoning, safety-critical systems—probabilistic pattern matching alone isn't enough. AGISystem2 provides:

  • Proof traces: Every conclusion traces back to facts and rules (audit logging/export is external)
  • Contradiction detection: The system flags inconsistencies in knowledge
  • Reproducible results: Same input always produces same output
  • Domain modularity: Semantic libraries can be composed and reused

Scope & Honesty

What AGISystem2 does well:

  • Taxonomic reasoning (IS_A hierarchies, transitive inference)
  • Rule-based inference with backward chaining
  • Explicit contradiction detection within formalized domains
  • Proof traces for every conclusion (audit logging/export is external)

What AGISystem2 does NOT do:

  • Full natural language understanding or open-ended chat; the focus is on structured reasoning patterns and theory execution
  • Automatically ‚Äúknowing‚Äù new domains without modeling effort
  • Replacing domain science: UTE requires explicit theories and evidence, not vibes

Fundamental limitation: The system reasons correctly within a theory, but cannot guarantee the theory itself is correct. See The Symbol Grounding Problem for an honest discussion of this epistemological limitation.

A Long-Term Bet: Domain Semantic Libraries

UTE will not emerge from a single giant model. It will likely be built as a growing ecosystem of domain libraries: executable theories, evaluation suites, and evidence conventions. This is hard work and it needs a community.

  • Vocabulary: domain terms with operational meaning (pragmatics)
  • Rules & mechanisms: what follows from what, and why
  • Evaluation: benchmarks that make progress measurable (and prevent regressions)

If you want to contribute: build a small theory + tests, not a marketing claim. That‚Äôs how UTE gets real.

Philosophy: Theories as Tools

AGISystem2 adopts a pragmatic stance: theories are tools, not truths. The system guarantees correct reasoning within a theory, not the correctness of the theory itself. This isn't a limitation—it's how all formal systems work, from mathematics to legal codes.

The value is practical: transform natural language specifications into formal theories that can be analyzed, debugged, and revised. The consequences of your knowledge become computable, verifiable, and auditable.
