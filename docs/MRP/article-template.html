Overview

Replace this section with the opening of the article. It should state the problem clearly, explain why the topic matters, and establish the tension that the article will work through.

Publishing note: Update the page metadata, Open Graph tags, section anchors, and the article card in the MRP index when you publish a real article.

Main Claims

Use this section for the core thesis and its main supporting arguments.

Suggested structure

  • State the strongest constructive claim first.
  • Separate object-level claims from claims about frames, methods, or limits.
  • Use subsections when the article has multiple tensions that must be kept distinct.

Risks and Boundaries

Most MRP articles should explicitly state their own failure modes. If a framework can be misunderstood, abused, or stretched beyond usefulness, say so directly.

Implications

Explain what follows if the article is right, and where the claim should remain limited or provisional.

Closing

End with a restrained conclusion. MRP articles should aim for clarity under pressure, not rhetorical finality.
