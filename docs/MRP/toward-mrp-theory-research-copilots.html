Toward an MRP Theory of Research Copilots

Orchestrating interpretive regimes in scientific AI.

Author: Sînică Alboaie Series: Meta Rational Pragmatics

Overview

Scientific research is better described as an organized succession of local interpretive regimes than as a single homogeneous reasoning process. This article proposes a compact theoretical framework for research copilots grounded in Meta-Rational Pragmatics (MRP).

Scientific Heterogeneity

Success in one part of the scientific workflow does not transfer automatically to another. While retrieval-augmented systems improve synthesis, verification of scientific claims remains substantially harder. The internal heterogeneity of scientific work suggests that generic language-based intelligence is insufficient for end-to-end scientific reasoning.

Interpretive Regimes

We introduce the notion of interpretive regime to capture local differences without reifying them into disconnected faculties. A regime is a bounded mode of inquiry with its own epistemic objects, transformation rules, and validation procedures. Research proceeds through these regimes, and a useful copilot must manage transitions between them, preserving the validity of intermediate epistemic artifacts.

Orchestration Architecture

A research copilot should not be organized around a single generic reasoning loop. It should be a regime-sensitive orchestration system that identifies the current mode of inquiry, mobilizes domain-specific background knowledge, and translates intermediate results into forms validated by subsequent regimes. This is an architectural and methodological proposal for restrained, implementable scientific AI.

Conclusion

The contribution of an MRP framing is to provide a principled way to organize existing methods. It does not aim to replace existing models, but to structure them into an architecture where regime sensitivity and auditable transitions replace the assumption of universal model sufficiency.
