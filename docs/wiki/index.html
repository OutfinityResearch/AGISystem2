This small wiki collects the theoretical and algorithmic concepts that underpin AGISystem2. Each page connects philosophical and mathematical ideas to the concrete way the system implements geometric reasoning, layered theories, and value-aware control.

The index below groups concepts into a few families: reasoning styles, knowledge and values, geometric foundations, and a short technical note on how these ideas show up in the implementation.

Reasoning and Logic

Concept Description
Abduction Reasoning to the best explanation: starting from observations and inferring the most plausible causes.
Induction Generalising from examples into rules and new concepts by enveloping points in the conceptual space.
Deduction Deriving necessary conclusions from premises, seen as checking inclusion between conceptual volumes.
Analogy Transferring relations between domains by applying learned vector “deltas” between concepts.
Non-Monotonic Logic Reasoning that allows withdrawing conclusions when new information or context changes appear.
Counterfactuals “What-if” questions evaluated through temporary theories and alternative layers.
Deontic Logic Modelling obligations and permissions in the axiological space with forbidden and obligatory volumes.
Narrative Consistency Keeping stories and scenarios coherent over time and aligned with normative theories.
Symbolic Execution Exploring all feasible branches of a symbolic program or theory to find contradictions and counterexamples.
Abstract Interpretation Static analysis via controlled abstractions, used to guarantee properties without exhaustive exploration.
Expert Systems Rule-based predecessors of our engine, with explicit knowledge and traceable explanations.

Knowledge, Values and Governance

Concept Description
Conceptual Spaces Representing knowledge as points and volumes in a continuous multidimensional space.
Ontology The factual layer of dimensions and relations that structures the objective world inside the system.
Axiology The layer of values and moral judgements, geometrically separated from ontology for audit and bias control.
Symbol Grounding Anchoring symbols in experience and geometry instead of manipulating bare strings.
Pragmatics How context and speaker intent influence the interpretation of facts and questions.
Bias Systematic preferences or discrimination that can appear in data or theories and must be controlled explicitly.
Veil of Ignorance Rawlsian principle for “fair” scenarios where decision makers do not know their social position.
Auditability The ability to reconstruct and verify every ingestion and reasoning step through logs and versions.
Trustworthy AI The set of criteria (determinism, transparency, validation) that make the system acceptable in critical domains.

Geometric and Algorithmic Foundations

Concept Description
Hyperdimensional Computing Representing information via superposition, masking and permutations in a high-dimensional vector space.
Locality-Sensitive Hashing (LSH) Probabilistic techniques for fast nearest-neighbour search in high-dimensional spaces.
Permutation-Based Binding Using deterministic permutations of dimensions to bind subjects, relations and objects into composite vectors.
Masked L1 Distance A distance measure with masks that ignore or reweight subspaces (ontology vs. axiology).
Bounded Diamonds The canonical shape of concepts: intersections of axis-aligned boxes and L1 balls with relevance masks.
Theory Layers Knowledge layers that can be activated, compared and stacked to handle context and contradictions.
Validation Formal and empirical checking of answers and theories, including systematic search for counterexamples.

AGISystem2 Concepts

Concept Description
Sys2DSL The deterministic DSL used to express facts, questions, theory programmes and session scripts inside AGISystem2.
Memory Management Usage tracking, forgetting mechanisms, protection, and priority boosting for concepts.
