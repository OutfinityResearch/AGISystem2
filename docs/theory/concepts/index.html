Theoretical Concepts

Home · Theory · Concepts Mathematical foundations explained

This section provides detailed explanations of the mathematical and theoretical concepts underlying AGISystem2. Each page is designed to be accessible to software engineers who may not have a formal background in abstract algebra or information theory.

Algebraic Foundations

GF(2) - Galois Field

The algebraic structure with two elements (0 and 1) that makes XOR operations self-inverse. Foundation for reversible binding in HDC.

XOR Operations

Bitwise exclusive-or and its properties. Why XOR is the natural choice for holographic binding.

Similarity & Distance

Jaccard Similarity

Set-based similarity measure: intersection over union. Used by SPHDC for comparing sparse polynomial representations.

Hamming Distance

Bit-difference measure for binary vectors. Used by Dense-Binary HDC for similarity computation.

Hashing & Sketching

Hash Functions (DJB2)

Deterministic vector generation from concept names. Enables reproducibility without explicit storage.

Min-Hash

Locality-sensitive hashing for efficient Jaccard estimation. Enables SPHDC sparsification while preserving similarity.

Bloom Filters

Space-efficient probabilistic set membership. Used for fast rejection of non-matching candidates.

Vector Operations

Binding Operations

Creating associations between concepts. XOR for dense-binary, Cartesian XOR for SPHDC.

Bundling Operations

Superposing multiple vectors into one. Majority vote for dense-binary, set union for SPHDC.

Information Theory

Quasi-Orthogonality

Why random high-dimensional vectors are almost orthogonal. The blessing of dimensionality.

Capacity Analysis

How many concepts can be stored? Signal-to-noise ratios and graceful degradation.
