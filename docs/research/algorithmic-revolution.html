The Algorithmic Revolution

From Dense Matrix Multiplication to Probabilistic Sparsity.

1.1 The SLIDE Algorithm: Sub-Linear Deep Learning Engine

Developed at Rice University, SLIDE challenges the assumption that training requires calculating the output of every neuron. It uses Locality Sensitive Hashing (LSH) to identify only active neurons for a given input.

  • O(1) Complexity: Reduces complexity from linear O(N) to essentially constant time searches.
  • CPU Advantage: CPUs excel at the random memory access and branching required for hash table lookups, which would stall a GPU's SIMD architecture.

1.2 ThirdAI and the BOLT Engine

ThirdAI commercialized SLIDE through BOLT, a production-grade engine for Dynamic Sparsity. It allows training billion-parameter models on commodity CPUs.

  • PocketLLM: Training and fine-tuning directly on laptops or x86 servers.
  • Privacy: On-premise training enables "air-gapped" data privacy for sensitive sectors.

1.3 Neural Magic: DeepSparse and vLLM

Neural Magic, born from MIT, focuses on Sparsification, pruning networks by 80-90% without losing accuracy. Following their 2025 acquisition by Red Hat, they have pivoted to contributing these optimizations directly to vLLM.
