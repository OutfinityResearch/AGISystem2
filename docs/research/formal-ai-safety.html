Formal AI Safety

Hard-coding alignment through mathematical constraints.

Beyond "RLHF"

Most current alignment techniques rely on Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback (RLHF), which is essentially statistical "politeness." Formal AI safety instead uses Formal Methods to prove that an agent's behavior stays within safe bounds.

Core Concepts

  • Safe Reinforcement Learning: Modifying the objective function to include hard constraints that the agent cannot violate during exploration.
  • Certified Robustness: Providing mathematical proofs that a model's output will not change drastically under small perturbations.
  • Shielding: An external "System 2" monitor that intercepts unsafe actions proposed by a "System 1" neural network and replaces them with safe alternatives.

System 2 Verification

In AGISystem2, safety is not a "vibe"—it is a Formal Specification. We use languages like TLA+ to model safety properties and ensure that our agentic workflows are inherently aligned with human intentions by design.
