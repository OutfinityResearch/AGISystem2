Active Inference

Minimizing surprise through the Free Energy Principle.

What is Active Inference?

Popularized by Karl Friston, Active Inference is a theory from theoretical neuroscience that explains how biological systems maintain their integrity and exhibit purposeful behavior. It posits that agents act to minimize "variational free energy," which is mathematically equivalent to minimizing surprise or prediction error.

Core Concepts

  • World Models: Agents maintain a generative model of their environment.
  • Bayesian Belief Updating: Perceptions are updates to internal beliefs based on sensory evidence.
  • Action as Inference: Agents act not just to reach goals, but to gather information that reduces uncertainty (Epistemic Value).

Alignment with AGISystem2

Active Inference provides the mathematical framework for our System 2 vision. It offers a way to move from "reactive" token prediction to "proactive" agentic behavior that values curiosity, exploration, and long-term goal satisfaction.
