Decentralized AI (DeAI)

Removing the gatekeepers: AI on distributed infrastructure.

What is DeAI?

Decentralized AI refers to the training, deployment, and usage of AI models on a peer-to-peer network rather than a few massive data centers. This aligns with our principle that concentrating control reduces systemic resilience.

Core Initiatives

  • Bittensor: A decentralized machine learning protocol that incentivizes the production of machine intelligence through a marketplace of subnets.
  • Petals (GitHub): Collaborative inference and fine-tuning of large models (like Llama 2/3) across standard consumer hardware.
  • Federated Learning: Training models across multiple decentralized devices or servers holding local data samples, without exchanging them.

Relevance to System 2

DeAI provides the Governance layer for our agents. By using decentralized consensus protocols, we can ensure that an agent's "beliefs" and "decisions" are validated by multiple independent nodes, approximating the Epistemic Redundancy required for rigorous research.
