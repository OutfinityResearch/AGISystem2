Conceptual Spaces

The geometric bridge between symbolic logic and neural associations.

What are Conceptual Spaces?

Proposed by Peter Gärdenfors, Conceptual Spaces represent information geometrically rather than through symbolic rules or neural activations alone. Information is organized into domains (like color, shape, or weight) where concepts are regions defined by similarity and convexity.

The Intermediate Layer

  • Symbolic Level: High-level logic and language.
  • Conceptual Level: Geometric spaces where similarity is distance.
  • Sub-symbolic Level: Raw neural network activations.

Relevance to System 2

Conceptual spaces provide a way to perform Semantic Reasoning without the rigidity of classical logic. By using Voronoi diagrams and prototypicality, an agent can reason about "vague" concepts while maintaining a structural consistency that raw neural networks lack.

Alignment with VSA/HDC

We explore the mapping of Conceptual Spaces into Vector Symbolic Architectures. This allows us to perform geometric reasoning (System 2) using extremely efficient bitwise operations on CPU.
