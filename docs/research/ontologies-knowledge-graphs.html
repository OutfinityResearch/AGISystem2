Ontologies & Knowledge Graphs

Structuring the world's knowledge for machine-readable common sense.

What are Ontologies?

An ontology is a formal naming and definition of the categories, properties, and relations between the concepts that exist for a particular domain of discourse. In the context of AGISystem2, ontologies provide the "Common Sense" anchor for our agents.

Core Initiatives

  • Cyc: The world's longest-lived AI project, aiming to codify general common-sense knowledge into a massive logic-based engine.
  • WordNet: A large lexical database of English, where nouns, verbs, adjectives and adverbs are grouped into sets of cognitive synonyms (synsets).
  • SUMO (Suggested Upper Merged Ontology): The largest open-source ontology in existence, mapped to WordNet.

Knowledge Graphs & the Semantic Web

Technologies like RDF and SPARQL enable the linking of data across the web. We use Knowledge Graphs to ground LLM outputs in verifiable facts, reducing hallucinations by checking claims against a structured graph of entities.

Strategic Value

Ontologies provide the Deterministic Verification layer for System 2. By mapping an agent's reasoning path to an upper ontology like SUMO, we can formally prove whether a chain of reasoning is logically consistent with established facts.
