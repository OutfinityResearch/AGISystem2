Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KAN)

An interpretable and efficient alternative to Multi-Layer Perceptrons.

Moving Beyond MLPs

Multi-Layer Perceptrons (MLPs) have been the backbone of deep learning for decades. However, Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks (KAN), proposed by researchers at MIT and Caltech, introduce a radical change: they place learnable activation functions on the edges (weights) rather than the nodes (neurons).

Advantages over traditional Deep Learning

  • Interpretability: KANs can often discover the underlying mathematical formulas of a dataset (Symbolic Regression).
  • Efficiency: They can achieve better accuracy than MLPs with far fewer parameters.
  • Continuous Learning: KANs are less prone to catastrophic forgetting, making them ideal for long-lived agents.

Alignment with AGISystem2

The interpretability of KANs makes them a strong candidate for Auditable AI. Being able to "read" the activation functions as mathematical formulas allows for a level of verification that is impossible with standard black-box neural networks.
