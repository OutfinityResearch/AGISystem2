Explainable AI (XAI)

Creating trust through transparency and auditable reasoning.

Why XAI?

As AI is deployed in high-stakes environments (finance, medicine, law), the "black box" nature of deep learning becomes a liability. XAI aims to provide humans with a clear understanding of why a model made a specific decision.

Core Techniques

  • Post-hoc Interpretability: Using tools like SHAP or LIME to explain existing models.
  • Intrinsically Interpretable Models: Building models that are readable by design, such as Decision Trees or KANs.
  • Reasoning Traces: Generating human-readable rationales alongside every agent action.

Integration in System 2

Explainability is not just a feature—it is a requirement for System 2 Engineering. Every decision made by an AGISystem2 agent must be accompanied by an auditable trace that links back to formal specifications and evidence.
