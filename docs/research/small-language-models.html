Small Language Models (SLMs)

Proving that "bigger" is not always better for intelligence.

The Rise of SLMs

While the industry focuses on trillion-parameter models, a parallel trend is perfecting models with fewer than 3 billion parameters. These models, such as Microsoft Phi-3, TinyLlama, and Google Gemma 2B, often outperform much larger models on reasoning tasks through high-quality data curation.

Key Techniques

  • Knowledge Distillation: Training a smaller "student" model to mimic the behavior of a larger "teacher" model.
  • Textbook Quality Data: Using synthetic, perfectly structured data to teach reasoning rather than raw web-scrapes.
  • Low-Bit Training: Integrating schemes like BitNet early in the training process.

Strategic Goal

SLMs are essential for our PocketLLM vision. They allow us to deploy "reasoning kernels" on local CPUs, ensuring that an agent's System 2 functions are always available, even without internet access or high-end GPUs.
