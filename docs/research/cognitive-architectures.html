Cognitive Architectures

Merging symbolic and sub-symbolic layers into a unified mind model.

What is a Cognitive Architecture?

Cognitive architectures are frameworks that aim to model the full breadth of human intelligence. Unlike narrow AI models, they integrate perception, memory, reasoning, and motor control into a single system.

Classic Systems

  • SOAR: A symbolic architecture based on production rules and problem-solving through state-space search. It is one of the most mature models of general intelligence.
  • ACT-R: Developed by John Anderson, ACT-R models human cognition by combining symbolic production rules with sub-symbolic activation processes.
  • OpenCog (GitHub): An open-source project aimed at building a decentralized Artificial General Intelligence (AGI) based on the "AtomSpace" hypergraph.

Hybridizing with LLMs

We believe the future of System 2 lies in using LLMs as the "associative memory" and "language interface" while using a Cognitive Architecture like SOAR to maintain long-term goals, logical consistency, and deliberate planning.
