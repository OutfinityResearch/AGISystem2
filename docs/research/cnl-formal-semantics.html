CNL & Formal Semantics

Eliminating ambiguity in human-agent communication through structured subsets of natural language.

The Problem of Ambiguity

Natural language is inherently "lossy" and ambiguous. Large Language Models (System 1) are excellent at parsing this ambiguity but struggle to map it to deterministic execution or formal logic without hallucinations.

Constrained Natural Language (CNL)

CNLs are subsets of natural languages with restricted grammar and vocabulary. They look like English but behave like a formal language (like SQL or Lisp). Key examples include Attempto Controlled English (ACE) and the Grammatical Framework (GF).

Formal Pragmatics: We don't just care about the definition of words (semantics), but how they are used in context to trigger actions. In AGISystem2, CNL serves as the "System 2" interface that validates agent intentions.

Executable Natural Language

By mapping CNL directly to Formal Semantics (e.g., mapping a sentence to a SPARQL query or a Lisp program), we achieve:

  • Auditability: Humans can read the "code" as plain English.
  • Zero Hallucination: If a sentence doesn't fit the CNL grammar, the agent cannot execute it.
  • Bi-directional Mapping: Converting raw data/logic back into human-readable, verifiable claims.

Research Directions

We are investigating the use of LLMs as "translators" from natural language to CNL, which is then processed by deterministic symbolic engines. This hybrid approach combines the fluency of AI with the rigor of formal verification.
