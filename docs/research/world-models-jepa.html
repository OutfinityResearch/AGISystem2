World Models & JEPA

Yann LeCun's vision for autonomous AI that learns, reasons, and plans.

The JEPA Architecture

Meta AI's Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture (JEPA) is a non-generative approach to AI. Instead of predicting pixels or tokens, JEPA learns to predict the latent representations (meanings) of missing parts of an input. This allows it to learn "World Models" without being bogged down by irrelevant details.

Key components of an Autonomous Agent

  • World Model: Predicts what will happen in the environment as a result of an action.
  • Cost Module: Evaluates how "bad" a predicted state is.
  • Actor: Proposes actions to minimize the cost predicted by the World Model.

World Models as System 2

At AGISystem2, we see World Models as the engine for mental simulation. Before an agent takes a high-stakes action, it can "run" the action through its internal world model to verify outcomes—a quintessentially System 2 function.
