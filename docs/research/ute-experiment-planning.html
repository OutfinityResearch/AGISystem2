UTE: Experiment Planning (Research)

Home · Research · UTE · Experiment Planning Plan experiments to reduce uncertainty under constraints
UTE is meant to be an engine for “theories that get better”. Experiment planning turns uncertainty into action: choose measurements or interventions that reduce uncertainty (or disambiguate mechanisms) while respecting constraints.

What exists today

  • Planning and CSP solving: DS16
  • Core verb/operator patterns for actions: DS07g

UTE requirement: experiments are information actions

Objective

Define information objectives (reduce entropy, distinguish hypotheses) as first-class planning goals.

Constraints

Plan under real constraints: cost, time, feasibility, ethics, safety.

Closed loop

Execute the experiment, ingest results as evidence, revise the model, and re-plan.

Proposed extensions (research direction)

  • Experiment operators with explicit preconditions, effects, and evidence output schemas.
  • Information gain estimation integrated with uncertainty semantics.
  • Batch planning for multi-step experimental protocols.
