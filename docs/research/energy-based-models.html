Energy-Based Models (EBMs)

Capturing dependencies by learning a scalar energy function.

What are Energy-Based Models?

Championed by Yann LeCun, EBMs represent a different approach to probabilistic modeling. Instead of predicting a probability distribution directly, an EBM learns a mathematical function that assigns a low "energy" value to configurations of variables that are compatible and a high "energy" value to those that are not.

Key Philosophy

  • Avoiding Normalization: Unlike traditional probabilistic models, EBMs do not require the calculation of a partition function (normalization constant), which is often computationally intractable.
  • Compatibility as Reasoning: Inference becomes an optimization problem: find the configuration of variables that minimizes the energy function.

Relevance to System 2

EBMs are naturally suited for Constraint Satisfaction. In our System 2 vision, energy functions can be used to validate if an agent's proposed action is "compatible" with high-level safety constraints or logical requirements.
