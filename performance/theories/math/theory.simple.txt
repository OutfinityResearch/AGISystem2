# Mathematics Theory - Simplified Natural Language
# One statement per line, simple grammar for translator

# Set Theory Axioms
Extensionality_Axiom: If all elements of set A are in set B and all elements of set B are in set A, then A equals B.
Empty_Set_Axiom: There exists a set with no elements called EmptySet.
Pairing_Axiom: For any two elements a and b, there exists a set containing exactly a and b.
Union_Axiom: If x is in A and A is in F, then x is in the union of F.
Power_Set_Axiom: If A is a subset of B, then A is in the power set of B.
Subset_Transitivity: If A is a subset of B and B is a subset of C, then A is a subset of C.
Element_Subset: If x is in A and A is a subset of B, then x is in B.
Intersection_Definition: If x is in A and x is in B, then x is in the intersection of A and B.
Complement_Definition: If x is in U and x is not in A, then x is in the complement of A with respect to U.
Set_Difference: If x is in A and x is not in B, then x is in A minus B.
De_Morgan_Union: The complement of A union B equals the intersection of complement A and complement B.
De_Morgan_Intersection: The complement of A intersection B equals the union of complement A and complement B.
Distributive_Union_Intersection: A union (B intersection C) equals (A union B) intersection (A union C).
Distributive_Intersection_Union: A intersection (B union C) equals (A intersection B) union (A intersection C).
Cartesian_Product: If a is in A and b is in B, then the ordered pair (a,b) is in A cross B.

# Number Theory - Peano Axioms
Peano_Axiom_1: Zero is a natural number.
Peano_Axiom_2: If n is a natural number, then the successor of n is a natural number.
Peano_Axiom_3: Zero is not the successor of any natural number.
Peano_Axiom_4: If successor of m equals successor of n, then m equals n.
Peano_Induction: If P holds for zero, and P for n implies P for successor of n, then P holds for all natural numbers.
Addition_Zero: n plus zero equals n.
Addition_Successor: n plus successor of m equals successor of (n plus m).
Addition_Commutativity: If a and b are natural numbers, then a plus b equals b plus a.
Addition_Associativity: If a, b, c are natural numbers, then (a plus b) plus c equals a plus (b plus c).
Multiplication_Zero: n times zero equals zero.
Multiplication_Successor: n times successor of m equals n plus (n times m).
Multiplication_Commutativity: If a and b are natural numbers, then a times b equals b times a.
Multiplication_Associativity: If a, b, c are natural numbers, then (a times b) times c equals a times (b times c).
Distributive_Law: a times (b plus c) equals (a times b) plus (a times c).
Less_Than_Transitivity: If a is less than or equal to b and b is less than or equal to c, then a is less than or equal to c.
Antisymmetry: If a is less than or equal to b and b is less than or equal to a, then a equals b.
Trichotomy: For natural numbers a and b, exactly one holds: a less than b, a equals b, or a greater than b.
Division_Algorithm: For natural a and b with b greater than zero, there exist q and r such that a equals b times q plus r and zero less than or equal to r less than b.
Prime_Definition: A natural number p greater than one is prime if its only divisors are one and p.
Fundamental_Theorem_Arithmetic: Every natural number greater than one has a unique prime factorization.
GCD_Commutativity: gcd of a and b equals gcd of b and a.
GCD_Divides: The gcd of a and b divides both a and b.
Bezout_Identity: For natural numbers a and b, there exist integers x and y such that gcd(a,b) equals a times x plus b times y.
Coprime_Definition: Two numbers are coprime if their gcd equals one.

# Euclidean Geometry
Euclid_Postulate_1: Through any two distinct points, there exists exactly one line.
Euclid_Postulate_2: Any line segment can be extended indefinitely.
Euclid_Postulate_3: Given a center and radius, a circle can be drawn.
Euclid_Postulate_4: All right angles are congruent.
Euclid_Postulate_5: Through a point not on a line, exactly one parallel line exists.
SSS_Congruence: If three sides of triangle ABC equal three sides of triangle DEF, then the triangles are congruent.
SAS_Congruence: If two sides and the included angle of triangle ABC equal two sides and the included angle of triangle DEF, then the triangles are congruent.
ASA_Congruence: If two angles and the included side of triangle ABC equal two angles and the included side of triangle DEF, then the triangles are congruent.
Triangle_Angle_Sum: The sum of the three angles of any triangle equals 180 degrees.
Pythagorean_Theorem: In a right triangle, the square of the hypotenuse equals the sum of squares of the two legs.
Converse_Pythagorean: If in a triangle the square of one side equals the sum of squares of the other two sides, the triangle is a right triangle.
Thales_Theorem: An angle inscribed in a semicircle is a right angle.
Inscribed_Angle_Theorem: The central angle is twice the inscribed angle subtending the same arc.
Parallel_Transversal: When a transversal crosses parallel lines, alternate interior angles are equal.
Triangle_Inequality: The sum of any two sides of a triangle is greater than the third side.
Midpoint_Theorem: The segment joining midpoints of two sides of a triangle is parallel to the third side and half its length.
Triangle_Area: The area of a triangle equals half the base times the height.
AA_Similarity: If two angles of one triangle equal two angles of another, the triangles are similar.
Similar_Triangles_Ratios: In similar triangles, corresponding sides are proportional.
Circle_Area: The area of a circle equals pi times the radius squared.
Circle_Circumference: The circumference of a circle equals two times pi times the radius.

# Group Theory
Group_Closure: If G is a group and a, b are in G, then a times b is in G.
Group_Associativity: If G is a group and a, b, c are in G, then (a times b) times c equals a times (b times c).
Group_Identity: For every group G there exists an identity element e such that e times a equals a times e equals a.
Group_Inverse: For every element a in group G, there exists an inverse a^(-1) such that a times a^(-1) equals the identity.
Abelian_Group: A group is Abelian if a times b equals b times a for all elements.
Subgroup_Criterion: A non-empty subset H of group G is a subgroup if for all a, b in H, a times b^(-1) is in H.
Lagrange_Theorem: The order of any subgroup divides the order of the group.
Coset_Cardinality: All cosets of a subgroup have the same cardinality.
Normal_Subgroup: A subgroup N is normal if left cosets equal right cosets.
First_Isomorphism_Theorem: The quotient of G by the kernel of a homomorphism is isomorphic to its image.
Cyclic_Group_Generator: A cyclic group is generated by a single element.
Cyclic_Groups_Abelian: Every cyclic group is Abelian.
Ring_Additive: The additive structure of a ring forms an Abelian group.
Ring_Distributive: In a ring, multiplication distributes over addition.
Field_Inverse: In a field, every non-zero element has a multiplicative inverse.
Vector_Space_Closure: In a vector space, the sum of two vectors is a vector.
Scalar_Multiplication: In a vector space, a scalar times a vector is a vector.
Linear_Independence: Vectors are linearly independent if no non-trivial linear combination equals zero.
Basis_Definition: A basis is a linearly independent spanning set.
Dimension_Theorem: All bases of a finite-dimensional vector space have the same cardinality.

# Logic and Proof Theory
Modus_Ponens: If P is true and P implies Q is true, then Q is true.
Modus_Tollens: If not Q is true and P implies Q is true, then not P is true.
Hypothetical_Syllogism: If P implies Q and Q implies R, then P implies R.
Disjunctive_Syllogism: If P or Q is true and P is false, then Q is true.
Constructive_Dilemma: If P or Q is true, P implies R, and Q implies S, then R or S is true.
Double_Negation_Elimination: Not not P implies P.
Double_Negation_Introduction: P implies not not P.
De_Morgan_Logic_1: Not (P and Q) equals (not P) or (not Q).
De_Morgan_Logic_2: Not (P or Q) equals (not P) and (not Q).
Contraposition: P implies Q is equivalent to not Q implies not P.
Excluded_Middle: For any proposition P, P or not P is true.
Conjunction_Introduction: If P is true and Q is true, then P and Q is true.
Conjunction_Elimination: If P and Q is true, then P is true and Q is true.
Disjunction_Introduction: If P is true, then P or Q is true.
Universal_Instantiation: From for all x P(x), conclude P(a) for any a.
Universal_Generalization: If P(a) holds for arbitrary a, then for all x P(x).
Existential_Instantiation: From exists x P(x), conclude P(c) for some witness c.
Existential_Generalization: From P(a) for some a, conclude exists x P(x).
Reductio_Ad_Absurdum: If assuming P leads to contradiction, then not P.

# Real Analysis
Limit_Definition: f(x) approaches L as x approaches a if for every epsilon there exists delta such that |x-a| less than delta implies |f(x)-L| less than epsilon.
Continuity_Definition: f is continuous at a if the limit of f at a equals f(a).
Continuous_Sum: The sum of continuous functions is continuous.
Continuous_Product: The product of continuous functions is continuous.
Intermediate_Value_Theorem: If f is continuous on [a,b] and y is between f(a) and f(b), then f(c) equals y for some c in [a,b].
Extreme_Value_Theorem: A continuous function on a closed interval attains its maximum and minimum.
Derivative_Definition: The derivative is the limit of the difference quotient as h approaches zero.
Differentiable_Implies_Continuous: If f is differentiable at a, then f is continuous at a.
Power_Rule: The derivative of x^n is n times x^(n-1).
Product_Rule: The derivative of f times g equals f' times g plus f times g'.
Quotient_Rule: The derivative of f divided by g equals (f' times g minus f times g') divided by g squared.
Chain_Rule: The derivative of f composed with g equals f'(g(x)) times g'(x).
Mean_Value_Theorem: If f is continuous on [a,b] and differentiable on (a,b), then f'(c) equals (f(b)-f(a))/(b-a) for some c in (a,b).
Rolle_Theorem: If f is continuous on [a,b], differentiable on (a,b), and f(a) equals f(b), then f'(c) equals zero for some c in (a,b).
FTC_Part_1: If f is continuous, then the integral function is differentiable with derivative f.
FTC_Part_2: The definite integral of f from a to b equals F(b) minus F(a) where F is an antiderivative.

# Probability Theory
Probability_Axiom_1: The probability of any event is between zero and one.
Probability_Axiom_2: The probability of the sample space is one.
Probability_Axiom_3: For disjoint events, P(A union B) equals P(A) plus P(B).
Complement_Rule: P(not A) equals one minus P(A).
Addition_Rule: P(A union B) equals P(A) plus P(B) minus P(A intersection B).
Conditional_Probability: P(A given B) equals P(A and B) divided by P(B).
Multiplication_Rule: P(A and B) equals P(B) times P(A given B).
Independence_Definition: A and B are independent if P(A and B) equals P(A) times P(B).
Bayes_Theorem: P(A given B) equals P(B given A) times P(A) divided by P(B).
Total_Probability: P(A) equals sum of P(A given Bi) times P(Bi) over a partition.
Expected_Value_Linearity: E(X plus Y) equals E(X) plus E(Y).
Expected_Value_Scaling: E(c times X) equals c times E(X).
Variance_Formula: Var(X) equals E(X squared) minus E(X) squared.
Variance_Scaling: Var(c times X) equals c squared times Var(X).
Chebyshev_Inequality: P(|X minus E(X)| greater than or equal to k times sigma) is at most 1 over k squared.
Law_Large_Numbers: The sample mean converges in probability to the expected value.
Central_Limit_Theorem: The standardized sum of iid random variables converges to standard normal.
